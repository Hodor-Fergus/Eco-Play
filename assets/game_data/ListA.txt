Switch to LED Bulbs: Replace incandescent bulbs with LED lights, which use less energy and last longer.

Unplug Devices: Unplug chargers and electronic devices when not in use to prevent phantom energy loss.

Use Energy-Efficient Appliances: Invest in appliances with high energy efficiency ratings.

Optimize Thermostat Settings: Set your thermostat to a lower temperature in winter and a higher temperature in summer.

Seal Windows and Doors: Use weather stripping or caulk to seal gaps around windows and doors to prevent heat loss.

Use Power Strips: Connect multiple devices to a power strip and turn it off when devices are not in use.

Wash Clothes in Cold Water: Use cold water for laundry to save energy on heating water.

Dry Clothes Naturally: Air-dry clothes on a clothesline or drying rack instead of using a dryer.

Insulate Your Home: Proper insulation can reduce heating and cooling costs by keeping your home at a consistent temperature.

Install a Programmable Thermostat: Automate temperature adjustments based on your schedule to avoid unnecessary heating or cooling.

Limit Space Heater Use: Use space heaters sparingly and only in occupied rooms.

Utilize Natural Light: Open curtains during the day to use natural sunlight instead of artificial lighting.

Cook with Lids On: When cooking, keep lids on pots to reduce cooking time and energy use.

Turn Off Lights: Always turn off lights when leaving a room.

Reduce Water Heater Temperature: Set your water heater to 120°F (49°C) to save energy.
